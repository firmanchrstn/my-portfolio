UI/UX CASE STUDY

Mobile JKN Redesign

Transformasi pengalaman digital untuk layanan kesehatan masyarakat yang lebih inklusif dan efisien.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

3 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Background

Mobile JKN serves as the digital backbone of Indonesia’s National Health Insurance (BPJS Kesehatan), catering to over 200 million users. While functionally comprehensive, the application faces significant challenges in managing its vast administrative data. As an Information Systems student, I initiated this redesign project to address the growing "Complexity Gap"—where bureaucratic density often hinders the speed and ease of healthcare access. The goal was to transform a complex administrative tool into a seamless, intuitive digital health assistant.

The Challenge (Problem Statement)

Public service applications often suffer from "Information Overload." Through my analysis, I identified three core pain points:

  • Cognitive Overload: Dense administrative data regarding registration, rights, and obligations made it difficult for users to find relevant information during urgent situations.
  • Perceived Performance Friction: A cluttered and unorganized layout created a perception of a "heavy" and slow application, increasing user anxiety.
  • Weak Information Hierarchy: Critical data points, such as digital membership status and ID numbers, were often buried under secondary information.

The Solution (Design Strategy)

My approach focused on Simplification through Systemic Logic. I implemented several key UX strategies:

  • Modular Information Architecture: I restructured the linear data into a Card-Based UI. This allows users to scan essential information much more efficiently.
  • Progressive Disclosure: By utilizing Tabs and Accordions, I ensured that technical details are only visible when needed.
  • Visual Trust & Identity: The Digital Participant Card was redesigned with high-contrast visual cues for instant verification.
  • Empowering Self-Service: I optimized the FAQ and Service Channels to boost User Autonomy.

Design Process (Methodology)

I followed a structured design thinking process to ensure the solution meets both user needs and business goals:

  • Empathize: Analyzing user pain points in navigating public service bureaucracy.
  • Define: Mapping out the Information Architecture (IA) to identify data bottlenecks.
  • Ideate: Sketching modular layouts that prioritize speed and reliability.
  • Prototype: Developing high-fidelity designs in Figma with a clean medical aesthetic.

Key Features Highlights

  • Unified Dashboard: A personalized greeting with a high-priority "Get in Line" (Queue) action button.
  • Smart Filter Articles:Categorized health news to improve user engagement and education.
  • Ideate: Sketching modular layouts that prioritize speed and reliability.
  • Simplified Permissions:Transparent onboarding for location access to ensure a seamless "Check-in" experience at clinics.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates that digital transformation in public services is not just about aesthetics—it is about Information Resilience. By applying UI/UX principles to complex Information Systems, we can build a more inclusive and reliable healthcare ecosystem for all citizens.
