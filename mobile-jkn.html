Personal Project • UI/UX Redesign

Modernizing Mobile JKN: Bridging the Gap Between Administrative Complexity and User Experience.

Transforming public healthcare services into an inclusive, responsive, and user-centric platform for all Indonesians.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

4 Weeks

Tools

Figma, FigJam

Background

Mobile JKN serves as the digital backbone of Indonesia’s National Health Insurance (BPJS Kesehatan), catering to over 200 million users. While functionally comprehensive, the application faces significant challenges in managing its vast administrative data. As an Information Systems student, I initiated this redesign project to address the growing "Complexity Gap"—where bureaucratic density often hinders the speed and ease of healthcare access. The goal was to transform a complex administrative tool into a seamless, intuitive digital health assistant.

The Challenge (Problem Statement)

Public service applications often suffer from "Information Overload." Through my analysis, I identified three core pain points:

1. Cognitive Overload: Dense administrative data regarding registration, rights, and obligations made it difficult for users to find relevant information during urgent situations. 2. Perceived Performance Friction: A cluttered and unorganized layout created a perception of a "heavy" and slow application, increasing user anxiety. 3. Weak Information Hierarchy: Critical data points, such as digital membership status and ID numbers, were often buried under secondary information.

The Solution (Design Strategy)

My approach focused on Simplification through Systemic Logic. I implemented several key UX strategies:

Modular Information Architecture: I restructured the linear data into a Card-Based UI. This allows 1. users to scan essential information (like billing or facility locations) much more efficiently. 2. Progressive Disclosure: By utilizing Tabs and Accordions, I ensured that technical details are only visible when needed. This keeps the interface clean and approachable for non-technical users. 3. Visual Trust & Identity: The Digital Participant Card was redesigned with high-contrast visual cues for the "Active" status, ensuring instant verification at healthcare facilities. 4. Empowering Self-Service: I optimized the FAQ and Service Channels to boost User Autonomy, reducing the burden on physical help centers.

Design Process (Methodology)

My approach focused on Simplification through Systemic Logic. I implemented several key UX strategies:

Empathize: Analyzing user pain points in navigating public service bureaucracy. Define: Mapping out the Information Architecture (IA) to identify data bottlenecks. Ideate: Sketching modular layouts that prioritize speed and reliability. Prototype: Developing high-fidelity designs in Figma, focusing on a clean, modern medical aesthetic.

Key Features Highlights

1. Unified Dashboard: A personalized greeting with a high-priority "Get in Line" (Queue) action button. 2. Smart Filter Articles: Categorized health news to improve user engagement and education. 3. Simplified Permissions: Transparent onboarding for location access to ensure a seamless "Check-in" experience at clinics.

Conclusion & Takeaway

This project demonstrates that digital transformation in public services is not just about aesthetics—it is about Information Resilience. By applying UI/UX principles to complex Information Systems, we can build a more inclusive and reliable healthcare ecosystem for all citizens.
