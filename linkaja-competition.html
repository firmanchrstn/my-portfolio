Overview

The rebirth of LinkAja was initially conceived as a competition project developed by our team, UXORA, through the Multimedia In Action (MIA) 2025 competition held by UPN ‘Veteran’ Jakarta. The competition commenced on 29 October 2025, allowing us to explore the world of UI/UX Design extensively.

Even though we decided to withdraw midway through the competition to ensure that we could remain focused on our academic responsibilities and exams, I was determined to complete the entire process from research to feature ideation. This project is my dedication to producing design solutions that are not only aesthetically pleasing but also data-driven and fully user-oriented.

The Problem

Despite being a strategic collaboration of State-Owned Enterprises (BUMN), LinkAja faced significant user experience hurdles:

  • Lowest Industry Rating: Despite high download volumes and the most reviews on the Google Play Store, LinkAja recorded the lowest average rating compared to its competitors.
  • Poor Novelty Score: Research using the User Experience Questionnaire (UEQ) placed LinkAja’s "novelty" in the "Bad" category—among the bottom 25% globally.
  • Visual Complexity: Users frequently complained about a "cluttered" interface that was unintuitive and lacked accessibility for elderly or less tech-savvy users.
  • Missing Essentials: Critical features like biometric login (Fingerprint/FaceID) and easy phone number management were not optimally implemented.

The Solution: Four Pillars of Innovation

We introduced four key features to increase both the pragmatic and hedonic value of the application:

  • TapAja (NFC Payments): Streamlining transactions through contactless technology to match modern digital trends.
  • InvestasiAja (Digital Investment): Lowering the barrier to entry for micro-investments in gold and mutual funds.
  • ChatAja (AI Assistant): A responsive AI assistant capable of solving user issues instantly using natural language.
  • MainAja (Gamification): Engaging users through interactive games and a reward system (vouchers/balance) to boost retention.

Key Takeaways

This project highlighted the importance of balancing functionality with a "delightful" user experience. By simplifying the Information Architecture and introducing gamification, we aimed to turn a functional tool into an engaging financial ecosystem.

Design Focus: The ChatAja Experience

Disclaimer/Role Focus

While this project was a collaborative effort by UXORA, my primary focus and contribution in the prototyping phase centered on the ChatAja feature. To provide a clear and in-depth view of my design logic, this section will showcase the end-to-end user journey for the AI-powered assistant.

The Feature: ChatAja (AI-Powered Assistant)

ChatAja was designed to bridge the gap between complex financial services and user accessibility. It serves as a responsive, 24/7 technical support system that understands natural language.

Key Design Highlights

  • Instant Support:Eliminates the need for users to wait in phone queues or for slow email responses.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP):Designed to understand everyday conversational language, making it highly accessible for users who are not tech-savvy.
  • Proactive Problem Solving:Provides immediate solutions for common transaction issues or account inquiries.
  • Interactive Interface:A clean, chat-based UI that follows the "LinkAja Reborn" minimalist design language, ensuring a cohesive experience across the app.
